How Drowsiness Detection System Works

The Drowsiness Detection System is an innovative solution designed to prevent accidents caused by driver fatigue. It monitors the driver's facial landmarks in real-time, tracking eye closure and head movements to determine the driver's alertness level. When drowsiness is detected, the system triggers an alarm to alert the driver, preventing potential accidents.

The system operates in the following steps:

  1. Facial landmark detection: The system detects and tracks the driver's facial features, including eyes and head. It uses computer vision techniques to identify key facial landmarks.
  2. Eye closure monitoring: Continuous monitoring of the driver's eyes to detect any signs of drowsiness or fatigue. By analyzing changes in eye behavior, the system can determine if the driver is becoming drowsy.
  3. Head movement tracking: Tracking the driver's head movements to assess their alertness level. Sudden or erratic head movements can indicate drowsiness or loss of focus.
  4. Drowsiness detection and alarm triggering: If the system detects drowsiness based on eye closure and head movements, it triggers an alarm to alert the driver. The alarm can be a sound, vibration, or visual alert to ensure the driver is alerted.

The Drowsiness Detection System is crucial for ensuring road safety and preventing accidents caused by driver fatigue. It can be integrated into vehicles to provide real-time monitoring and alerts, helping to save lives and reduce the risk of accidents.
